About Me

I am an experienced human genomics scientist with in-depth knowledge of DNA sequencing data analysis who is especially interested in delivering high-quality results through reproducible, scalable bioinformatic pipeline development. My personal bioinformatics credo is "We are not software developers, but we do develop software." As such, I believe that borrowing from the field of software engineering is a necessity for bioinformaticians, and I require that my development teams implement best practices regarding version control, code review, and unit and black box testing. I am passionate about reproducibility: I use workflow management systems like Snakemake with conda and Singularity/Docker containers to manage workflows and environments, and Jupyter notebooks to record and communicate my findings.

Experience

Cancer Genomics Research Laboratory, NCI/Leidos Biomedical Research, Inc.

Bioinformatics Scientist

I work in a core sequencing facility that supports the ~60 investigators in the Division of Cancer Epidemiology and Genetics, NCI, NIH. I currently lead our microbiome pipeline development team, where I am instituting best practices including version control, code review, and unit/regression/black box testing. I have written a modular, platform-agnostic pipeline to coordinate structural variant calling using multiple containerized callers, then annotate and compare results across callers; this project was awarded $20,000 for development locally and on cloud platforms. I developed a pipeline to implement non-negative matrix factorization to detect mutational signatures in somatic sequencing data, which has revealed evidence for a novel risk factor in a specific cancer type via the signatures detected. I am currently working on an ensemble germline variant calling pipeline that can scale to large cohorts and can generate an output VCF that meets the specific requirements of our PIs. I have been invited to teach workshops on pipeline development with Snakemake and on data analysis with Python’s pandas package.

Personal Genome Diagnostics

Scientist, Genome Sciences

I analyzed next generation sequencing data to identify medically actionable somatic mutations in tumors, and generate reports for physicians, academic institutions, and pharmaceutical clients. I developed, implemented, and maintained an automated report generation tool, eliminating sources of manual error and reducing turnaround time for reporting NGS results from hours to seconds. I collaborated closely with software engineers to establish requirements, regression testing strategy, and user acceptance testing plans for CLIA reports and related software. I instituted and led weekly Genome Sciences team training sessions covering use of command line and NGS analysis tools to facilitate data analysis.

National Institutes of Health

Postdoctoral Fellow

I explored the molecular basis of cancer susceptibility through whole exome sequencing and SNP array genotyping of individuals with genomic instability syndromes, in both family- and population-based studies. In my position at the NIH, I was able to leverage biospecimens collected under the National Cancer Institute's longitudinal prospective cohort study "Etiologic Investigation of Cancer Susceptibility in Inherited Bone Marrow Failure Syndromes." I analyzed next generation sequencing and in silico data, and collaborated closely with molecular biologists, clinicians, epidemiologists, and bioinformaticians. This allowed me to examine germline and somatic alterations in individuals with disorders of aberrant telomere maintenance or DNA damage repair that result in bone marrow failure and extremely high risk of cancer. My work resulted in the discovery and publication of several novel gene-disease associations.
